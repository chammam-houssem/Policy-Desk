## mix

Algeria's electricity mix has evolved mainly through gas-system expansion rather than fuel diversification, with oil shrinking to a residual role and renewables still marginal. The main turning point was the renewables planning cycle launched in 2011 and updated in 2015, which framed renewables as a way to save natural gas while demand kept rising. Current signals point to gradual PV-led diversification, but the system remains structurally centered on gas.

**System profile:** Gas-dominated

- Fossil generation accounted for about 99% of electricity in 2023
- Renewables were about 2% of installed capacity but only 1% of generation
- Oil has declined steadily while gas remains the dominant fuel

**Main driver:** domestic fossil resource availability

**Trajectory:** Algeria is likely to stay gas-dominated in the near term while solar expands gradually through state-led procurement.

**Sources:** [IEA electricity](https://www.iea.org/countries/algeria/electricity); [IEA renewable plan update](https://www.iea.org/policies/6103-renewable-energy-and-energy-efficiency-development-plan-2015-2030?country=Algeria)

## solar-wind

Solar and wind still play a minimal role in Algeria's power system, with solar far ahead of wind and CSP increasingly de-prioritized. Deployment is moving toward utility-scale solar PV through the Solar 1000 and larger tender model, but implementation has been slower than official targets. The main limitation is execution capacity across procurement, delivery, and grid integration rather than resource potential.

**Status:** Project pipeline

- Solar provided most of the 926 GWh of renewable generation in 2023
- Installed renewables were led by solar at 462 MW, while wind remained at 10 MW
- Official plans shifted toward large-scale PV and delayed CSP because of cost

**Constraint:** limited implementation capacity

**Implication:** Solar is likely to grow first and shape Algeria's renewable path, but system impact will remain limited until tenders translate into operating capacity.

**Sources:** [IEA electricity](https://www.iea.org/countries/algeria/electricity); [IEA renewable plan update](https://www.iea.org/policies/6103-renewable-energy-and-energy-efficiency-development-plan-2015-2030?country=Algeria)

## hydrogen

Hydrogen is meant to carry Algeria's long-standing energy role into a cleaner era, but the transition might be slow. The strategy is in place, and the 50 MW Arzew pilot plus the SoutH2 Corridor are the clearest signs of intent, yet nothing has reached final investment decision. Real scale still rests on faster renewable growth and clearer rules.

**Status:** Early strategy

- National hydrogen strategy approved in 2022
- 50 MW Arzew pilot and SoutH2 Corridor feasibility work
- Strong link to solar buildout and ammonia exports

**Constraint:** Renewables

**Implication:** Algeria's hydrogen plan will remain hard to finance until renewable capacity grows enough to support green hydrogen instead of Blue or Grey Hydrogen.

**Sources:** [Ministry strategy](https://energypartnership-algeria.org/fileadmin/algeria/5JAAE-MEM.pdf); [Sonatrach ALTEH2A](https://sonatrach.com/en/sonatrach-strengthens-its-strategic-cooperation-with-its-european-partners-for-the-development-of-green-hydrogen/); [IRENA North Africa](https://www.irena.org/Publications/2025/Jun/Enabling-green-hydrogen-development-North-Africa)
