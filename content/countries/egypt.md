## mix

Egypt's mix expanded mainly through natural gas as demand rose, while hydro stayed broadly flat and lost share. The mid-2010s shortage response, backed by tariff reform and a large gas-fired build-out, locked gas in as the system backbone. Current plans point to gradual diversification through renewables, storage, and grid reinforcement rather than a rapid fossil exit.

**System profile:** Gas-dominated

- Gas supplies roughly three-quarters of generation
- Hydro is stable in output but smaller in share than in 2000
- Wind and solar are growing, but fossil generation still dominates

**Main driver:** energy security

**Trajectory:** Egypt will likely remain gas-backed in the near term while renewables scale inside a thermal system.

**Sources:** [IEA](https://www.iea.org/countries/egypt/electricity); [Egypt NDC](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2023-06/Arab%20Republic%20of%20Egypt%20Updated%20NDC.pdf)

## solar-wind

Solar and wind now have a visible but still secondary role in generation. Deployment has shifted from flagship projects such as Benban and Gabal El-Zeit to a larger pipeline tied to batteries and grid reinforcement. The binding condition is system integration, not resource availability.

**Status:** Accelerating

- Solar and wind reached about 2.5% and 3.1% of generation in 2023
- Benban and Gabal El-Zeit anchored utility-scale build-out
- NWFE links new solar additions to battery storage and network upgrades

**Constraint:** grid integration

**Implication:** Solar and wind can keep expanding, but their impact depends on transmission, storage, and offtake readiness.

**Sources:** [IEA](https://www.iea.org/countries/egypt/electricity); [NWFE](https://moic.gov.eg/page/109)

## hydrogen

Hydrogen sits near the center of Egypt's export story, anchored in SCZONE and the Suez Canal. The policy framework is more mature than in most peers and one project is operating, but much of the pipeline still lives at the MoU or pre-FEED stage. The real test is whether finance and grid upgrades can catch up with the ambition.

**Status:** Project pipeline

- National Low-Carbon Hydrogen Strategy launched in 2024
- Egypt Green is partially operational
- Strong linkage to ammonia, bunkering, and renewables

**Constraint:** financing

**Implication:** Egypt could strengthen its regional export role, but only if bankable contracts and grid capacity improve.

**Sources:** [National strategy](https://sis.gov.eg/en/media-center/strategies/national-strategy-for-low-carbon-hydrogen/); [Egypt Green project](https://sis.gov.eg/en/media-center/news/pm-reviews-status-of-100-mw-green-hydrogen-production-project-in-sczone/); [EU-Egypt partnership](https://north-africa-middle-east-gulf.ec.europa.eu/news/eu-and-egypt-launch-initiatives-sustainable-energy-cooperation-2026-02-10_en)
