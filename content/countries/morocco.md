## mix

Morocco's energy mix has diversified since the 2009 strategy, but coal still dominates generation even as renewable capacity expanded. The driver for RE adoption was import dependence and subsidy pressure, which pushed the country to adopt RE for energy security and diversification. The near-term trajectory remains a coal-backed transition until storage, grids, and replacement capacity scale further.

**System profile:** Coal-heavy transition

- Coal still provides around 60-62% of generation
- Renewables are close to half of installed capacity but far lower in generation
- Hydro output has weakened as drought reduced reservoir levels

**Main driver:** energy security

**Trajectory:** Morocco will keep diversifying through solar, wind, and storage while coal remains central in the near term.

**Sources:** [IEA](https://www.iea.org/countries/morocco/electricity); [MASEN](https://www.masen.ma/en/projects)

## solar-wind

Solar and wind are central to Morocco's diversification, with wind already contributing materially and solar shifting toward PV plus storage. Deployment is moving through MASEN-led utility projects, repowering, and larger solar-plus-battery tenders, while self-generation is also expanding. The binding limit is transmission and system integration as variable capacity grows faster than the grid.

**Status:** Scaling

- Wind contributes about 15% of generation and solar about 4.8%
- Noor Midelt II and III moved to PV plus battery storage
- Self-generation solar expanded quickly by mid-2025

**Constraint:** transmission infrastructure

**Implication:** Solar and wind will deepen the transition, but generation dominance depends on storage and north-south grid expansion.

**Sources:** [MASEN](https://www.masen.ma/en/projects); [World Bank](https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099081425124511419/pdf/P511426-583aaa13-1c09-4fd7-98e2-66d4471815a3.pdf)

## hydrogen

Hydrogen is central to Morocco's industrial and export strategy, with OCP giving the story a strong domestic anchor. The policy frame is well developed, and projects like Chbika and Jorf are moving beyond MoUs, but real scale still depends on export contracts and shared infrastructure arriving on time. It remains the region's clearest export-oriented case.

**Status:** Scaling / export-oriented

- Offre Maroc and MASEN single-window framework
- OCP green ammonia and Jorf platform advancing
- Chbika and other consortium projects selected for development

**Constraint:** demand uncertainty

**Implication:** Morocco is closest to turning hydrogen into a real export industry, but only if European offtake contracts materialize.

**Sources:** [MASEN offer](https://www.masen.ma/en/green-hydrogen-moroccan-offer); [OCP green investment](https://ammoniaenergy.org/organization/ocp/); [Chbika project](https://totalenergies.com/news/press-releases/renewables-green-hydrogen-te-h2-cip-and-ap-moller-capital-partner-large-scale)
