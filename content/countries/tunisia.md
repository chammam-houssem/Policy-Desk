## mix

Tunisia's mix has become more gas-dependent since 2000 as domestic production declined and imports rose. The post-2011 policy reset and the 2015 renewable law marked the main turning point, but the system still remains dominated by STEG and gas. Current plans point to diversification, yet the near-term system stays structurally gas-heavy.

**System profile:** Gas-dominated

- Natural gas still supplies about 94-95% of generation
- Renewable capacity is rising faster than renewable generation share
- STEG remains dominant in both capacity and generation

**Main driver:** energy security

**Trajectory:** Tunisia is likely to remain gas-heavy near term while utility-scale renewables and grid upgrades gradually expand.

**Sources:** [IEA](https://www.iea.org/countries/tunisia/electricity); [World Bank](https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099042925180525062/pdf/P507304-7dbd5265-7691-4a38-8755-12a36e4645e8.pdf)

## solar-wind

Solar and wind still have a limited role in generation, but Tunisia is moving into a more active utility-scale phase. Recent solar commissioning, signed wind PPAs, and 2026 tenders show stronger implementation after years of delay. The main limitation is the grid's ability to absorb new variable capacity.

**Status:** Accelerating

- Renewables were about 6% of generation with 787 MW installed by mid-2025
- The Metbasta plant marked a utility-scale breakthrough
- Tunisia approved roughly 2 GW of wind and 350 MW of solar tenders for 2026

**Constraint:** grid integration

**Implication:** Solar and wind should gain a larger role, but their contribution depends on transmission, storage, and smoother project execution.

**Sources:** [IEA](https://www.iea.org/countries/tunisia/electricity); [World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/news/press-release/2025/11/11/world-bank-approves-new-project-to-power-tunisia-s-energy-transformation)

## hydrogen

Hydrogen is presented as a future export opening for Tunisia, but the gap between vision and delivery is still wide. The national roadmap exists and several MoUs have been signed, yet no FID or operating electrolyser is in place. The central constraint is governance, since Tunisia still lacks the legal and regulatory base for implementation.

**Status:** Early strategy

- National green hydrogen strategy published in 2024
- Gabes pilot is the main near-term project
- Multiple MoUs, but no construction or FID

**Constraint:** governance

**Implication:** Tunisia is unlikely to move beyond pilots until the legal framework and investment conditions become clearer.

**Sources:** [MIME strategy](https://www.energiemines.gov.tn/fileadmin/docs-u1/Re%CC%81sume%CC%81_strate%CC%81gie_nationale_MIME_Anglais.pdf); [GIZ PtX study](https://www.giz.de/en/downloads_els/GIZ%20PtX%20Tunisia%20report-Web.pdf); [SoutH2 Corridor](https://www.south2corridor.net/)
